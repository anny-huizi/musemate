Starry Night

Artist: Vincent van Gogh

Year: 1889

Medium: Oil on canvas

Location: Museum of Modern Art, New York

What inspired this painting?

Vincent van Gogh painted Starry Night during his stay at the asylum of Saint-Paul-de-Mausole near Saint-RÃ©my-de-Provence. The night sky depicted in the painting suggests his continuing preoccupation with the imagination and dreams, despite his mental health struggles.

The swirling patterns in the sky reflect van Gogh's emotional state and his unique artistic vision. The painting combines observational elements with imaginative interpretations, creating a scene that feels both familiar and otherworldly.

Art historians believe the painting represents both van Gogh's view from his asylum window and elements from his memories and imagination, synthesized into one of the most recognizable masterpieces in Western art.
